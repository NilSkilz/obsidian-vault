> Fellow rigger here who went through the exact same thing when I joined my local scene. A few things that helped me break in:
>
> **Workshops over munches (at first):** Structured activities where you're learning alongside others are so much easier than open mingling. You've got a built-in conversation topic, and people naturally pair up for practice. Plus you walk away with a skill.
>
> **Look for other newbies:** At most events there's usually at least one or two other people standing awkwardly alone. They're in the same boat - go introduce yourself. Some of my best scene friendships started from bonding over "we have no idea what we're doing."
>
> **Pick one community and commit:** Instead of spreading yourself thin across multiple events, pick one munch or one play party and become a regular. People need to see your face 3-4 times before they start treating you like a familiar presence rather than "who's that new person."
>
> **FetLife follow-up:** After an event, find people you talked to on FetLife and connect there. Easier to continue conversations online, and it builds that consistency between events.
>
> The cliquey thing is frustrating, but it's usually not personal - people just default to their comfort zones. Give it 2-3 months of consistent attendance at the same events and it'll shift. You're not too late - you're just early in your journey.
