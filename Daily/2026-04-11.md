### 2) “autistic and new to the scene, questions” (r/BDSMcommunity)
- URL: https://www.reddit.com/r/BDSMcommunity/comments/1sikfae/autistic_and_new_to_the_scene_questions/
- Mode: Markdown

> Couple extra bits from a neurodivergent rigger:
>
> - For your first event, pick a munch or workshop. It’s way lower pressure than a play party and you can get a feel for the vibe.
> - Message the organisers *before* you go. Ask about dress code, consent policies, quiet spaces, and whether there are dungeon monitors. Good organisers are happy to answer.
> - Consent revocation is simple: say “red/stop” (or the venue’s safeword), and the scene ends. If you can’t speak, agree on a hand signal/tap-out beforehand. If someone ignores that, you stop and find a DM/organiser immediately.
> - Sensory stuff: earplugs are normal, sunglasses are fine, and it’s okay to step outside. Most folks get it if you just say “overstimulated, need a minute.”
> - Don’t feel like you have to play with anyone. Watching and learning is valid.
>
> Also, don’t answer random DMs from this post. Meet people in public spaces first, and trust your gut.
>
> You’re asking the right questions — take it slow and you’ll be fine.
