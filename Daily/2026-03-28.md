A few things I've learned the hard way:

Practice on yourself first. Seriously. Tie your own leg, your own wrist to a chair. You'll learn how rope feels when it's too tight, when nerves are getting pinched, and how quickly things can go from "fine" to "numb."

Start with single and double column ties - they're the foundation of everything. Get those solid before moving to anything decorative.

Have a circulation check routine. Every few minutes: ask how they're feeling, check their fingers (colour, warmth, mobility). Make it part of your scene rhythm.

Since you mentioned arms above head - be extra cautious with that one. It limits blood flow pretty quickly, so shorter duration at first. Keep checking in.

And get good rope! 6mm jute or hemp for beginners works great. Avoid anything slippery like nylon for ties that need to hold.
