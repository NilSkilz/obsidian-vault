The biggest thing is to ask HER what her little headspace is like. Littles vary wildly - some regress to toddler age, some to teen, some are bratty, some are shy. There's no one-size-fits-all approach.

That said, some general tips:

- Use a softer, warmer tone than your normal voice
- Praise works wonders - "good girl," "you're doing so well," "I'm so proud of you"
- Simple choices feel safe - "do you want juice or milk?" rather than open-ended questions
- Have age-appropriate activities ready (coloring books, stuffies, cartoons, snacks)
- Set clear, simple rules and boundaries - littles often feel safer with structure

For language, avoid anything that feels condescending or mocking. The goal is nurturing, not patronizing. Think protective parent energy, not talking down to someone.

Most importantly - have a conversation BEFORE she's in little space about what she needs, her triggers, and how to help her come out of headspace safely afterward. Aftercare is just as important with age play as with anything else.
